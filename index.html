Who we are

A movement rooted in second chances

NayePankh — meaning "new wings" — began in 2014 with a single after-school classroom in a resettlement colony on the outskirts of Delhi. Today, we run learning centres, nutrition programs, and mentorship circles across four states.

We believe that a child's circumstances at birth should never determine their ceiling. Our volunteers, teachers, and partners work alongside families to remove the barriers — financial, social, and emotional — that keep children from school.

Every program we run is built on one idea: give a child the right support early, and they will carry themselves the rest of the way.

What we do

Programs built around real needs

Each program addresses a specific gap families told us mattered most — not what looked good on a brochure.

Learning Centres

After-school classrooms offering remedial education, reading support, and digital literacy for children aged 6–14.

Nutrition & Health

Daily meals, growth monitoring, and basic healthcare check-ups so hunger and illness never stand in the way of school.

Mentorship Circles

College students and young professionals mentor teens through career guidance, scholarship applications, and life skills.

Family Outreach

Home visits and community workshops that help parents support learning and access government welfare schemes.

Girl Child Education

Targeted scholarships, escort programs, and family counselling to keep girls enrolled through secondary school.

Vocational Training

Skill-building workshops in tailoring, computer basics, and trades for youth transitioning out of school.
